3-Day Routine
Strength · Mobility · Golf
Mobility
Strength
Core
Lower body + hip mobility
~40 min  ·  Golf swing focus
Hip mobility and glute activation — the foundation of your swing. The most important session of the three.
Warm-up · 10 min
90/90 hip switches Watch
5 each side × 2 sets — rotate between internal and external hip positions on the floor
The single best move for golf hip rotation. Slow and deliberate.
World's greatest stretch Watch
5 each side — lunge + thoracic rotation + reach (Squat University)
Combines hip flexor, hip mobility and upper body in one. Don't rush it.
Hip circles on all fours
10 each direction, each hip — draw big slow circles with your knee
Opens the joint capsule. Foam roller nearby if your knees need padding.
Lateral band walks Watch
Band around knees, side-step 10 each way × 2 sets
Activates glute med — key for hip stability through the downswing.
Strength · 20 min
Goblet squat Watch
3 sets × 10–12 reps · one dumbbell held at chest · moderate weight
Spine-friendly squat. Keep chest tall, heels grounded throughout.
Single-leg Romanian deadlift Watch
3 sets × 8 each side · one light-moderate dumbbell · balance-focused
Use a wall for support if needed — no shame at all starting that way.
Glute bridge Watch
3 sets × 15 reps · bodyweight or dumbbell on hips · pause 2 sec at top
L5-S1 safe and excellent for the posterior chain. Feel your glutes, not your back.
Cool-down · 5 min
Foam roller — glutes and hip flexors
1–2 min each side · slow rolling · pause on tight spots
Worth spending the full time here. These muscles did the work.
Mobility
Strength
Upper body + thoracic mobility
~40 min  ·  Stiffness focus
Thoracic spine work to open your rotation and reduce that top-of-swing stiffness. Upper body strength to match.
Warm-up · 10 min
Thoracic rotation — seated Watch
10 each direction × 2 sets — hands behind head, rotate slowly through mid-back
The big unlock for your upper body. Focus on the t-spine, not the lower back.
Foam roller thoracic extension Watch
Roller across mid-back, hands behind head, extend over it — 8 positions along the spine, 3 reps each
Do NOT roll onto your lumbar spine. Stop at the waistline.
Cat-cow + thread the needle Watch
10 cat-cows, then 8 thread-the-needle each side — follow-along video
Just press play and move with it. Great for shoulder and upper back stiffness.
Strength · 20 min
Dumbbell incline press Watch
3 sets × 10–12 reps · moderate weight · 45° incline · control the descent
Chest and shoulders without heavy spinal load.
Single-arm dumbbell row Watch
3 sets × 10–12 reps each side · bench supported · pull elbow to hip · spine neutral
Rowing is vital for posture and swing plane.
Band pull-apart Watch
3 sets × 15 reps · arms forward at shoulder height, pull band to chest
Counters forward posture from desk work. Rear delts and upper back.
Dumbbell lateral raise
2 sets × 12 reps · light weight · controlled · avoid shrugging
Shoulder stability matters in the golf swing.
Cool-down · 5 min
Doorway chest stretch
45 sec each arm · 2 positions (elbow low + elbow high)
Most desk workers are chronically tight here. Hold, breathe, release.
Mobility
Strength
Core
Full body + core
~40 min  ·  Integration session
Ties everything together. Lighter loads, movement quality focus, and the anti-rotation core work your swing demands.
Warm-up · 8 min
90/90 + thoracic rotation combo Watch
5 each side — rotate the hip, then add upper body rotation over the front knee
Mimics the hip-trunk separation your swing needs.
Lunge with torso rotation Watch
8 each side — step forward, rotate upper body over front leg, step back
Golf-specific pattern. Rotate from t-spine, not the lower back.
Strength · 18 min
Dumbbell Romanian deadlift Watch
3 sets × 10 reps · two legs · moderate weight · hinge at hips, soft knees
Stop when you feel the hamstrings — not the lower back.
Push-up (elevated if needed)
3 sets × 8–15 reps · hands on bench for easier variation
Adjust to your energy that day. Bench elevation makes it very manageable.
Band face pull Watch
3 sets × 15 reps · anchor band at face height · pull to ears · elbows high
Rear delt and rotator cuff health. Critical for shoulder durability in golf.
Core · 10 min
Dead bug Watch
3 sets × 8 each side · slow and controlled · lower back pressed flat to floor (NASM)
The safest and most effective core exercise for L5-S1. No crunching whatsoever.
Plank
3 × 20–40 sec · forearms · neutral spine · don't hold your breath
Short holds done well beat long holds done badly. Stop before form breaks.
Pallof press (band) Watch
3 × 10 each side · anchor band at chest height · press straight out · hold 2 sec
Anti-rotation training — exactly what your core needs to do in the swing.
Note on the ab roller: Hold off for now — it loads the lumbar spine significantly. Once your core baseline is solid after 8–10 weeks, you can start with short roll-outs from your knees.
General approach: Aim for 2 min rest between strength sets. If a session feels like too much on a given day, drop one strength exercise — the mobility work is non-negotiable. Rest at least one day between sessions. Sessions should feel manageable, not punishing.
